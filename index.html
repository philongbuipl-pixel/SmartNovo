Good to know.

The things operators and brands ask us first.

How fast can a unit open?
It is craned onto a prepared site and trading in under an hour. Because the unit is legally classed as a vending machine, there is no lease, no build-out and no six-month consent process.
Do you store faces or use facial recognition?
No. The vision system reads body movement through 138-point skeletal tracking, never a face. It is identity-free by architecture, which keeps it aligned with RODO and the EU AI Act by design.
What hardware does it need?
Commodity cameras at the edge. There is no specialist hardware to buy, and the same model handles entry, theft, falls, inventory and checkout.
Can it run under our own brand?
Yes. The 21 m² unit is the same underneath; the outside is fully customisable with your own design and wrap, and the in-store screens carry your promotions and ads. Convenience, pharmacy, campus, forecourt or resort.
How many people does it take to operate?
One. A single assistant services up to five containers, and the AI dashboard tells them exactly what to bring and to which store. Everything else, from checkout to ordering, the AI handles itself.
What does a site need?
Just land. The unit connects to grid power or runs on optional solar panels, internet comes via Starlink, and fridges and freezers run inside, so even remote locations work.
Can we license just the vision, without a container?
Yes. Theft and fall detection can run on the cameras you already have, with no container required.
Can a unit be moved later?
Yes. The steel unit weighs about 3.5 tonnes, so it loads onto a small truck and is road-legal, with the stock still on the shelves. If a location underperforms, the store reopens somewhere better in under an hour. You never lose the store.
