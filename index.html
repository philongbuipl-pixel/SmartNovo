The technology

Vision that reads movement, never faces.

138-point skeletal tracking and behaviour recognition, built in-house over four years and running on commodity cameras at the edge.

Live · theft detection

Concealment, flagged in real time.

The model reads the gesture of a product going into a pocket or bag. In the same second the customer gets a message that an unpaid item was detected and should be returned, while the operator is alerted.

  • Behaviour recognition trained on theft gestures, not identities
  • Runs on commodity cameras at the edge, with no specialist hardware
  • Operator and deterrent alerts fire instantly

Questions

Good to know.

The things operators and brands ask us first.

How fast can a unit open?
It is craned onto a prepared site and trading in under an hour. Because the unit is legally classed as a vending machine, there is no lease, no build-out and no six-month consent process.
Do you store faces or use facial recognition?
No. The vision system reads body movement through 138-point skeletal tracking, never a face. It is identity-free by architecture, which keeps it aligned with RODO and the EU AI Act by design.
What hardware does it need?
Commodity cameras at the edge. There is no specialist hardware to buy, and the same model handles entry, theft, falls, inventory and checkout.
Can it run under our own brand?
Yes. The 21 m² unit is the same underneath; the brand on the outside is yours. Convenience, pharmacy, campus, forecourt or resort.
Can we license just the vision, without a container?
Yes. Theft and fall detection can run on the cameras you already have, with no container required.
Can a unit be moved later?
Yes. A unit relocates to a new site in under an hour as demand shifts, so a slow location is never a stranded asset.
